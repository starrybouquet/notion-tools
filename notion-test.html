It wasn't often SG-1 had so much downtime around Christmas. Though the halls of the SGC were often quieter as it neared December 25th, SG-1 had continued business as usual right up until Christmas Eve.

Not this year. This year, Daniel had begged to be allowed to go on a dig in Egypt the week before Christmas, and General O'Neill had agreed.

Teal'c had jumped at the chance to spend some extended time with Ry'ac, and so SG-1 was collectively on downtime until at least after Christmas.

Ordinarily, Sam loved Christmas. She loved the way the world seemed quieter in the winter, loved the traditional trimmings and decorations. And though she missed her mother more often and keener than usual during the holidays, Sam also felt closer to her when she put up her Christmas decorations than any other time of year.

But this year, her favorite part of Christmas was missing. Her family—the adopted one. The people she fought side-by-side with, the people she laughed and cried and fought and talked with. Really talked.

Oh, she had Janet and Cassie still. But Daniel and Teal'c wouldn't be back until Christmas Eve. Hammond, who usually came over for Christmas, was spending the holiday with his oldest son back in Texas.

"Hey, Carter." Her thoughts were interrupted by the low rumble of her CO's voice.

"Sir. I didn't know you were on base." Actually, she'd tried not to think about the fact that he was on base.

The General shrugged, oblivious to her musings. "Well, not much going on. I told Reynolds to take off early, said I'd cover his shift." He leaned on her lab table and gestured at the device she was working on. "Making a bomb, Carter?"

It did kind of look like one. "No, sir. It's something SG-8 brought back last week. Not sure what it is yet."

"Fascinating." O'Neill shoved his hands in his pockets. "Well, I have faith in you, Carter. Poke and prod away, as long as it's not at me..."

She turned her head to get at the device's control panel—getting dangerously close to the General's hip where it leaned against the table—and poked at what she thought was the power source.

Immediately, red lines arced from the periodic slits in the ball to her skin, and she let out a strangled sound of pain. "Ah!"

"Carter!" The General grabbed her arms and wrenched the ball from her hands. "Carter, you okay?"

Sam blinked hard and shook her head. "I'm fine."

"C'mon. Let's take you to the infirmary."

"Sir, it didn't hurt me, I don't need—"

"Ah! The Doc is in charge of that one." He steered her out of the lab.

The General kept up a quiet running commentary as they walked.

"Gotta do those requisition forms, Janet's going to be on me about that. Damn. Can't remember what she wanted. And there's those tac vests, and..." Sam appreciated the company, and dutifully half-listened to the surprsingly-detailed todo list he had for the rest of the day through the corridor and into the elevator.

"And SG-12 will be back less than an hour now," he was saying as they entered the elevator. "I should be in the control room to greet them. Not like Carter really needs me here anyway. It's more like I need to be here, to make sure she's okay."

"Sir?" Sam interrupted, surprised. They never talked about that, and certainly not recently.

"Carter?" He looked at her worriedly. "You good? You know you don't need to pretend in front of me."

She frowned at him, totally gobsmacked. "What did you just say?"

"I asked if you're OK?"

She shook her head. "You said I didn't need to pretend around you."

"What?!"

She frowned. "Wait. I think that device is a telepathy device."

"What?!" Gonna need more than that, Carter.

"I'm hearing you say things without moving your lips."

"Okay, sounds like telepathy to me."

It wasn't until Sam was hanging her coat up, thoughts loud in her too-quiet house, that she heard the crinkling in her pocket. Intrigued, she rooted around until she found a piece of paper, slightly bent.

Pulling off her boots, she made her way into the living room and sunk onto her couch to read it.

It was three pages, and though the paper was crisp, it was worn at the folds, as if someone had unfolded it and refolded it several times.

Sam,

You know that writing isn't really my thing. It makes you talk about feelings. Give me a gun and a target any day.

But I'm going to try this anyway, because I think I need to write these things down. And also because I'll probably never have the guts to say this out loud.

Sam, the past week has been one of the best weeks of my life. We don't talk about it, but you know I miss being in the field. And yeah, believe me, taking out a snakehead with a zat gun is so much easier than taking them out through a MALP radio. But what I actually miss most isn't the action.

It's you.

I think you might know this already. God knows the rest of the base does, despite everything I've tried to deny it to Walter, and you are a freakin' genius, after all. But in case you don't...

I miss you, Sam. When you're off world, when you're out on a date. Hell, when you leave the base and I'm still filling out requisitions for more size XL BDUs.

Like I said, this week has been one of the best weeks of my life. Definitely the best since Charlie.

It's a selfish dream, but I want more than a week. I'm still your CO and I don't have much to offer beyond some knees way past warranty, a sarcastic head tilt or two, and a full set of Simpsons DVDs. I don't know why I'm writing this—never got this far in the past. Hoping for a Christmas miracle, I guess. I'm running out of time, after all.

If you want, ignore this letter, and nothing will happen. I promise, Carter. Cross my heart. If nothing else, I will respect your wishes. But if you're still the slightest bit interested—

Maybe we could just...talk. Just Jack and Sam. No strings attached.

Merry Christmas.

Love,

Jack.
